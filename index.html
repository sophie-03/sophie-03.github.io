About Me

I am a soon-to-be PhD graduate in Genomic Data Science whose doctoral thesis investigates how variation in gene copy number influences cancer risk at two levels: within the human population, to identify direct functional associations, and across the mammalian phylogeny, to explore long-term evolutionary patterns. My expertise lies in large-scale genomic analysis, phylogenetic comparative methods, and evolutionary genomics. I am passionate about leveraging comparative evolutionary analyses to inform human health and am seeking a postdoctoral position that aligns with these research interests.
